Study Design Retrospective cohort of all patients ≥75 years admitted to TASMC (2006–2022). Chronic PPI use defined as listed daily PPI at admission. Patients with evidence-based indications for long-term PPI were excluded. Data extracted via MDClone platform.

Key Finding Both PPI (HR 0.55) and aspirin (HR 0.53) show a nearly identical “protective” association with 30-day mortality. This symmetry strongly suggests confounding by healthy-user bias rather than a true pharmacological effect.

Key Interpretation After PSM, infection ORs decreased by 20–32% but remained highly significant, strengthening the causal argument for PPI-infection risk. The 30-day mortality HR moved from 0.55 toward the null (0.72), confirming that ~50% of the apparent “protection” was attributable to measured confounding. The persistence of HR 0.72 suggests unmeasured confounders (functional status, frailty, goals of care) continue to bias the estimate.
